# Risks and Mitigations

# ThreadVault

> This section outlines key risks identified during ThreadVault v1 development and the mitigations in place to support a successful MVP launch.

### Risk: High onboarding friction

Uploading multiple clothing items may feel time-consuming, reducing initial engagement.

**Mitigation:** Keep v1 lightweight, allow incremental uploads over time, and avoid mandatory metadata beyond name and category.

### Risk: Incomplete wardrobe data limits perceived value

Users may not upload enough items to immediately experience the benefits of a digital closet.

**Mitigation:** Design the visual overview to remain useful even with partial wardrobes and highlight progress as users add items.

### Risk: Privacy concerns around clothing images

Users may hesitate to upload personal photos of their clothing.

**Mitigation:** Default all wardrobes to private, avoid social sharing in v1, and clearly communicate that images are user-controlled.

### Risk: Low return usage after initial setup

Users may upload items once but fail to return regularly.

**Mitigation:** Emphasize everyday utility through browsing, item counts, and visual reminders of underused clothing.

### Risk: Premature feature complexity

Introducing automation or social features too early could increase cognitive load and reduce usability.

**Mitigation:** Intentionally defer advanced features until core workflows and engagement are validated.

### Operational Considerations (v1)
- User-uploaded images are stored securely and remain private by default.
- No social or collaborative features are enabled in v1, minimizing moderation requirements.
- Core metrics focus on item uploads, wardrobe views, category usage, and return visits.
- Support expectations are minimal during MVP validation and limited to bug fixes and usability improvements.
- The system is designed to support incremental feature expansion without disrupting core wardrobe data.
